# VerityAir Systems Ltd Deployment Model

## Purpose

This note is the investor-facing summary of how VerityAir Systems Ltd intends to deliver VerityATLAS to customers.

It is written to support pitch conversations, investor diligence, and deck narrative.

## Short Answer

VerityATLAS is intended to be delivered first as a cloud-based multi-company platform, with higher-isolation deployment options for enterprise and defence customers.

In simple terms:

- primary model: `multi-tenant cloud SaaS`
- premium model: `single-tenant hosted`
- sensitive model: `private / defence deployment`

## Why This Matters

The product is being built for regulated operators who need:

- operational oversight
- mission assurance
- evidence continuity
- renewal and readiness support
- the ability to manage multiple teams, aircraft, missions, and records

That makes cloud delivery attractive for the commercial market, but it also means some customers will eventually require stronger hosting isolation.

## Primary Commercial Model

### Multi-Tenant Cloud SaaS

This is the recommended default commercial delivery route.

Why:

- fastest customer onboarding
- lowest deployment friction
- best fit for recurring SaaS revenue
- allows VerityATLAS to serve multiple companies and concurrent operations from one platform
- supports efficient product updates and feature rollout

Investor message:

VerityATLAS is designed to become a scalable assurance platform for many regulated operators, not a one-off bespoke deployment business.

Current build relevance:

- the backend now includes the first implemented tenant-isolation and authenticated membership controls across stored files, OA, insurance, organisation documents, mission governance, and risk-map routes
- this strengthens the credibility of the multi-tenant cloud direction, while still falling short of a finished production security posture

## Higher-Assurance Expansion

### Single-Tenant Hosted

This is the likely premium deployment option for larger enterprise and critical-infrastructure customers.

Why:

- stronger customer isolation
- easier procurement for high-assurance users
- supports enterprise accounts that are not comfortable with shared SaaS

Investor message:

This creates a higher-value commercial tier without changing the core product.

## Defence And Sensitive Delivery

### Private / Defence Deployment

This is the controlled later-stage option for defence, government, and restricted environments.

Why:

- some customers will require private cloud, sovereign hosting, or customer-controlled environments
- this supports dual-use expansion without forcing the commercial product to become defence-shaped too early

Investor message:

Defence-grade delivery can be a strategic expansion path, but it should follow commercial product maturity rather than replace it.

## Recommended Positioning

The clean investor line is:

VerityATLAS is being built as a multi-company assurance platform delivered through cloud SaaS first, with higher-isolation deployment options for enterprise and defence customers as the market expands.

## What To Say In A Pitch

- the default delivery route is cloud SaaS
- the platform is intended to support multiple operators and missions simultaneously
- enterprise and defence customers can be served later through isolated deployment models
- this preserves commercial scale while keeping strategic flexibility

## What Not To Overclaim

- do not imply that private or defence deployment is already productised unless it is
- do not suggest the platform is fully multi-tenant-hardened if that work is still in progress
- do not let defence hosting complexity dilute the primary commercial SaaS story

## Bottom Line

The deployment strategy supports both:

- commercial scalability
- higher-assurance expansion

That is important because it means VerityAir Systems Ltd can pursue a broad commercial market first, while retaining a credible path into enterprise and dual-use opportunities.
