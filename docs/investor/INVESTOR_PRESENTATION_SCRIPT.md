## Deck Script

### Slide 1. Title

On-slide:

- VerityAir Systems Ltd
- Platform: VerityATLAS
- Mission assurance software for regulated drone operations

Script:

VerityAir Systems Ltd is building VerityATLAS, an assurance-led software platform for regulated drone operations. The core idea is simple: help operators plan, assess, execute, monitor, and evidence missions through one connected operational record.

### Slide 2. Problem

On-slide:

- As operations scale, complexity rises
- Current operator pain
- Operators can fly the mission. They still struggle to prove the mission was safe, authorised, and accountable.

Script:

The problem is no longer just flight planning. As operators move into repeatable commercial work, more complex airspace, and BVLOS-adjacent operations, they face a growing assurance burden. Planning, readiness, approvals, live context, and evidence are often fragmented across point tools, spreadsheets, and judgement. Missions may still get flown, but proving what was checked, approved, and why is much harder than it should be.

### Slide 3. Why Now

On-slide:

- enterprise drone use is expanding
- BVLOS raises the assurance burden
- regulators, customers, and insurers want traceability
- dual-use demand is growing

Script:

This matters now because the category is maturing. Enterprise drone use is moving beyond simple VLOS jobs. BVLOS growth increases the need for structured safety and evidence workflows. At the same time, regulators, customers, and insurers are expecting more traceability, not less. That creates the opening for a more serious operational assurance layer.

### Slide 4. Solution

On-slide:

- VerityATLAS is an assurance-led FSMS platform for regulated UAS missions
- One assurance chain from mission intent to auditable evidence

Script:

VerityATLAS is designed to connect one mission record across planning, risk, airspace, platform readiness, pilot readiness, approval and dispatch, live operations, replay, and audit evidence. The point is not another planner or another checklist. The point is one assurance chain from mission intent to auditable evidence.

### Slide 5. Product Today

On-slide:

- Current product shape, grounded in the build

Script:

Today, the build already covers meaningful operational depth. That includes mission planning and approval handoff, lifecycle controls, mission risk, airspace compliance, platform readiness, pilot readiness, telemetry and replay, alerts and mission events, external overlays, traffic conflict assessment, audit evidence, and SMS framework mapping. More recently, the platform has added OA and insurance evidence handling, organisation document workflows, authenticated membership-based access, combined governance assessment, a risk-map early-warning surface, and the first OA personnel model including pending amendment logic.

### Slide 6. Why VerityATLAS Wins

On-slide:

- Not just a planner. Not just a checklist.
- The moat is not a single feature. It is the operational assurance chain.

Script:

Many competitors solve slices of the workflow. VerityATLAS is strongest where the buyer needs continuity, accountability, and evidence across the mission lifecycle. That is the moat. Not one feature, but the operational assurance chain from intent to approval, approval to dispatch, live ops to replay, and replay to evidence.

### Slide 7. Market Entry Wedge

On-slide:

- Audit-ready mission assurance inside an OA and insurance-controlled envelope
- Start where regulated operators already feel the pain

Script:

The opening wedge should stay disciplined. The best starting point is audit-ready mission assurance for commercial operators working inside an Operational Authorisation and insurance-controlled envelope. That pain is real, specific, and easy to explain. It also expands naturally into readiness, live ops, and evidence.

### Slide 8. Customer Segments

On-slide:

- Near-term operators
- Later expansion environments
- Start with serious operators. Expand later.

Script:

The near-term focus is on enterprise drone operators, infrastructure and inspection teams, emergency service drone teams, and other regulated commercial operators carrying OA, insurance, and audit burden. Later, the platform can expand into BVLOS programme operators, UTM-adjacent partners, and higher-assurance dual-use settings. The key is to start with serious operators who already need this discipline.

### Slide 9. Market Size

On-slide:

- commercial drone software is the most relevant TAM reference
- VerityATLAS is targeting the mission-assurance layer inside that market

Script:

We should be careful and credible with market size. The broad commercial drone market is large, but the more relevant top-down reference is commercial drone software. VerityATLAS is not trying to claim the whole drone economy. It is targeting the assurance, readiness, and evidence layer within that broader software market, which is a more believable and defensible position.

### Slide 10. Business Model

On-slide:

- SaaS subscription
- pricing by operator, fleet, mission volume, or assurance tier

Script:

The commercial model is SaaS. Over time that can price by operator, fleet, mission volume, or assurance tier, with onboarding and configuration around regulatory profile and operating workflow. The logic is straightforward: the more serious and regulated the operator, the more value there is in structured assurance and evidence continuity.

### Slide 11. Deployment Model

On-slide:

- Cloud-first for commercial scale
- Higher-isolation delivery where needed

Script:

VerityATLAS is being built cloud-first because that is the best route to commercial scale. The default model is multi-tenant SaaS, which supports multiple companies and concurrent operations on one platform. For higher-assurance enterprise customers, there is a clear single-tenant hosted path. And for more sensitive environments, there is a later private or defence deployment path. The important point is that we can scale commercially first without losing a credible route into higher-isolation markets later.

### Slide 12. Validation Plan

On-slide:

- design partners
- pilot deployments
- measured outcomes
- Turn product depth into measurable operator proof

Script:

The next stage is about proof. This product does not need a leap of faith so much as design partners, live deployments, and measured outcomes. The metrics that matter are time saved, fewer missing evidence items, fewer fragmented workflow steps, and stronger confidence in auditability. The job now is to convert build depth into operator proof.

### Slide 13. Go-To-Market

On-slide:

- founder-led sales into regulated operators
- Win credibility first. Scale from proof, not pitch alone.

Script:

Go-to-market should stay disciplined. Start with founder-led sales into serious regulated operators, not hobby markets. Use design partners to sharpen the wedge and produce measurable proof, then expand from readiness and evidence into broader mission assurance workflows.

### Slide 14. Market Map

On-slide:

- direct competitors
- adjacent competitors
- VerityATLAS as mission-assurance system of record

Script:

The market is not empty. There are credible players across operations management, compliance, airspace, fleet intelligence, and UTM-adjacent workflows. The difference is that most of them manage slices of the problem. VerityATLAS is being positioned as the mission-assurance system of record, especially for serious regulated operators who need evidence continuity and accountability.

### Slide 15. Competition Section Flow

On-slide:

- Market Map
- Competitor Reviews
- Competitor Scale Signals

Script:

This section works best as a progression. First, who is in the market. Second, what buyers appear to value. Third, who looks large publicly and why that still does not mean the assurance-led category is closed.

### Slide 16. Competition Framing

On-slide:

- categories, not only brand names
- Most alternatives solve slices of the workflow

Script:

When speaking about competition, the framing should stay category-led rather than name-led. The real alternatives include enterprise operations tools, compliance platforms, fleet intelligence tools, UTM and BVLOS-enablement systems, and in many cases internal spreadsheet and document processes. The key message is that most alternatives solve slices of the workflow, while VerityATLAS ties the mission assurance record together.

### Slide 17. Competitor Reviews

On-slide:

- What buyers value
- Public review signals
- Where VerityATLAS can win

Script:

Public review evidence in this sector is thinner than you might expect, but where it exists the pattern is useful. Buyers seem to reward ease of workflow, responsive support, visibility, and practical field usefulness. That matters because an assurance-led platform cannot become bureaucratic. VerityATLAS needs to win on trust and usability while still offering a stronger assurance chain, OA and insurance awareness, personnel-aware logic, and accountable-manager visibility.

### Slide 18. Competitor Scale Signals

On-slide:

- visible public scale
- metrics are mixed and not directly comparable
- the category is still open

Script:

There are real scale signals in the market. Some players report customers, others users, pilots, agencies, sites, or deployments. That shows maturity, but it does not settle category ownership. The more specific category VerityATLAS is targeting, assurance-led regulated mission operations with evidence continuity and accountable oversight, still lacks a clearly dominant platform.

### Slide 19. Founder-Market Fit

On-slide:

- military aerospace operations background
- civilian route planning and airline operations
- organisational capability and management grounding

Script:

Founder-market fit is strong here. The background behind VerityATLAS combines military aerospace operations, civilian airline and route-planning experience, and management grounding. That matters because this is not software trying to learn aviation. It is aviation and operational discipline being translated into software.

### Slide 20. Roadmap

On-slide:

- prove the wedge first
- expand the platform second

Script:

The roadmap should feel disciplined. Near term, the focus is customer proof, core hardening, wedge sharpening, multi-tenant and access-control hardening, and maturing OA personnel workflows in product. Later comes broader regulatory support, deeper live operations and advisory support, larger-scale organisational workflows, and selected higher-assurance extensions. The point is to prove the wedge first and expand the platform second.

### Slide 21. Ask

On-slide:

- customer proof
- early revenue
- product hardening
- pilot deployment support
- design partner onboarding
- early commercial execution

Script:

The ask is grounded. This is not capital for a slide deck concept. It is capital to convert a substantial build into live proof, early revenue, stronger deployment readiness, and a more durable commercial position. The build is ahead. The ask is to turn that into proof and traction.

## Delivery Notes

- Keep the commercial operator story first.
- Use defence only as an expansion path, not the opening claim.
- Do not overclaim regulator connection or legal automation where the product is still evidence-led.
- If challenged on scale, return to the wedge: assurance continuity for serious regulated operators.
- If challenged on competition, return to the moat: one operational assurance chain, not point tools.
